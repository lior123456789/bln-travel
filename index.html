The Process

Eighty-six steps.
Six weeks. One case.

01

Hand-drawn. Then proved.

Every silhouette starts on paper. Mock-ups are 3D-printed, weight-balanced, and dropped from six feet a hundred times before a millimeter is committed to metal.

02

Cut from a single sheet.

Each panel is CNC-milled from a single billet of 7000-series aluminum. The wave pattern is press-formed, preserving the alloy's structural integrity.

03

Leather, stitched by hand.

Full-grain vegetable-tanned leather from a fourth-generation Tuscan tannery. Four hours per case.

04

Eighty-four checks.

Wheel rotation. Latch tension. Hinge alignment. Seam pressure. Every case earns the BLN signature on the inner panel.
